Civil Cases: Plaintiff/Claimant - Defendant (accused)

Hearing commences when the prosecution calls his witnesses or opens his case in court. Note that when the prosecution fails to establish a prima facie case (on the face of it), the defence pleads a no-case submission.

Stages of Hearing

  1. Examination in Chief:
    • Done by the prosecution, i.e., the counsel who listed the witness out to give evidence in that suit.
    • Counsel calls out the witness and questions him/her.
    • Opportunity to tender documents, add up facts, and establish facts to be admitted.
    • Rules for document tendering:
      1. Lay a foundation.
      2. Relevance.
      3. Adherence to rules of admissibility.
      4. Must be pleaded.
    • No leading questions during examination in chief (Sec. 221(1&2) Evidence Act).
  2. Cross Examination:
    • Conducted by the counsel on the opposing side (defense).
    • Questions the witness of the opposite side (prosecution) based on the evidence given or the facts in issues.
    • Punctures, tests the veracity of the evidence/witness, and discredits the witness.
    • Leading questions can be asked here in criminal matters when the prosecution fails to establish a prima facie case against the defendant.
  3. Re-examination:
    • Done by the witness's counsel to the witness.
    • Corrects ambiguities from the examination in chief.

Final Written Address

This is the last thing you file to the court.

It is a document encompassing evidence with reasons why those evidence should be admitted and why any evidence should be dismissed, preliminary objections counter motions. It also has the counter motion.

The defendant files the final written address first within 21 days while the prosecution is given 7 days to respond to the defendant.

This is where all the given evidence is written down, why they were adopted by the court and/ dismissed. In Kalu v State final written address means the last or ultimate speech or submission made to the court in respect of the matter before it, before delivery of judgement.

Plea of Allocutus (tamper justice with mercy) a plea made before the court goes for recess and then gives final judgement and it is very different from guilty plea as plea of allocutus is done before sentencing while guilty plea is done after sentencing.

Judgment is the final decision while ruling is the adoption of exhibits in court. Judgment is final, once made cannot be brought back to the court because it is;

  1. Functus officio res judicata: Once a court has passed a valid sentence after a lawful hearing the case cannot be reopened. An exception to this is an appeal to the supreme court, the case becomes functus officio res judicata.
  2. Double jeopardy (autre fois convict, autre fois acquit): Autre fois convict; If a person has been convicted for a particular crime, one cannot sue that person for the same said crime again. Autre fois acquit; If a person has been acquitted for a particular case you cannot use that person for the court to acquit that person again.

Components Of A Written Address/ Final Written Address

  1. An introduction
  2. Brief summary of facts
  3. Issues for determination
  4. Legal arguments
  5. Conclusion

The prosecution responding to the written address responds on points of law, while responding;

  1. You do not argue facts.
  2. You do not bring up new issues.
  3. You do not raise issues.

Motions

A motion is an application before the court to grant certain reliefs. It is an application before a court praying the court for certain reliefs or remedies. Carries two addresses yours and that for service. Plaintiff/ Defendant Applicant ~ Plaintiff/ Defendant Respondent.

Types:

  • Substituted service
  • Bail
  • Injunction

A motion has three parts;

  1. Motion paper: founding document for your affidavit. Would be dated, have the statutory authority.
  2. Affidavit:
    • Introductory part - I, name, sex, adult, religion, citizenship, nativity, profession, do hereby make oaths and state as follows i.e description of the individual.
    • It is usually in numbered paragraphs.
    • Space for a deponent to sign (s.117 evidence Act; a person who deposes to an affidavit) after your averment.
    • Jurat- s.119 evidence Act applies in the case where an illiterate is involved (an illiterate is someone who does not understand the language in which he is spoken to). This jurat is to show that the jurat has explained the content of the affidavit to the deponent and he/she has understood and signed afterward. The commissioner for oaths is the registrar.
  3. Written Address: outlines the arguments

Motion on Ex Parte/Interim Injunction: This is a motion given without the notice of the other party. It refrains from fair hearing and therefore it becomes unconstitutional. One must convince the court to grant this motion and after granting, it lasts for 7 days. It is done before any trial and during legal proceedings. Once it has been granted, it becomes a motion on notice after which the other party would defend themselves. This motion is given to stop a person from doing a particular thing or a specific act. This motion is granted due to the urgency of the res within which a motion on notice is granted.

Montion on Notice/Interlocutory Injunction: The order given when a motion on notice is served and granted is called an interlocutory injunction and this lasts pending the determination of the substantive suit. Injunctions do not determine rights; if that should be then you would have to file a substantive suit, i.e., a main suit.

Substamtive Suit/Pepertual Injunction: During the main suit, one can then request for a perpetual injunction which is in continuum.

The statement given on the admissibility of various documents in between proceedings is a ruling. A motion is an originating motion.

Motion can be moved in terms or by outlining in details the different parts of the motion. Before you move a motion you must seek for leave of court to move that motion and the court must grant the leave before you go ahead. Moving in terms based on the terms of the motion paper...rely on your affidavit and adopt the written address.

Civil Proceeding

Four Ways to Institute a Civil Action

  1. Writ of Summons: Used when the matter involves contentious issues, i.e., issues where facts are disputed.
  2. Originating Summons: This is used when the matter involves non-contentious issues, i.e., issues where facts are not disputed but issues of law.
  3. Originating Motions: Used for fundamental rights enforcement claims or to seek a particular relief in court.
  4. Petitions: Used to institute certain kinds of matters, dissolution of marriage, winding up of companies, electoral matters.

Pleadings

Civil matters are fought basically on pleadings.

The person who institutes a civil matter is the plaintiff while who it's against is the defendant.

Reason why the court should not admit a case...Preliminary Objection. Defendant/Applicant

Plaintiff/Respondent

Statement of Claim is the first point of pleading for the applicant and the response to it is Statement of Defence. The plaintiff then replies to the statement of defence after which the defendant places a counter claim (at this point the burden of proof shifts to the defendant) and then the plaintiff replies to the counter claim.

Things That Accompany Writ of Summons

Plaintiff

  • Writ of summons
  • Statement of claim
  • List of witnesses
  • Written deposition
  • List of documents
  • Documents to be relied on

Defendant

  • Memorandum of appearance (conditional: appearing in protest /unconditional: full appearance)
  • Statement of defence/counter claim
  • List of witnesses
  • Written deposition
  • List of documents
  • Documents to be relied on

Hearing

This is the substance of every matter or suit. It encompasses three processes Examination-in-chief, Cross Examination, and Re-examination.

In civil processes, there is a thing called pre-trial conference. It is a mini-suit performed before the actual commencement of the main suit. It is done by filing a pre-trial information sheet with yes or no questions in order to hear from the clients about what actually caused the said matter and tries to limit the issues to a lesser number of issues, know the number of witnesses and the time allocated to each witness, set the modalities on how the matter would be proceeded afterward the matter goes into the hearing stage.
